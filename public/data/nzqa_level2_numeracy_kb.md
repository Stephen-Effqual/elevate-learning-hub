# NCEA Level 2 Numeracy Knowledge Base

## Overview

The NCEA Numeracy co-requisite ensures students can apply mathematics and statistics in everyday situations. From 2024, students must achieve 10 numeracy credits (plus 10 literacy credits) as a 20-credit co-requisite for any NCEA qualification.

## Numeracy Standard

### US32406: Use Mathematics and Statistics in a Range of Everyday Situations

**Credits:** 10  
**Level:** 1  
**Assessment:** External Common Assessment Activity (CAA)

#### Purpose:
Learners credited with this standard are expected to:
- Formulate mathematical and statistical approaches to solving problems in meaningful situations
- Use mathematics and statistics to meet numeracy demands of various contexts
- Explain the reasonableness of mathematical and statistical responses

#### Skills Required:

**Number Operations:**
- Fluently solve problems involving addition, subtraction, multiplication, division
- Understand relative size of numbers
- Interpret answers in context
- Work with fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Use ratios and proportions

**Mathematical Relationships:**
- Recognise and work with patterns
- Understand algebraic relationships
- Apply formulas to solve problems

**Spatial Properties:**
- Understand and use properties of shapes
- Work with representations of objects
- Apply geometric reasoning

**Location and Navigation:**
- Understand and use coordinate systems
- Read and interpret maps
- Apply navigation concepts

**Measurement:**
- Use appropriate units of measurement
- Measure with appropriate precision
- Convert between units
- Calculate perimeter, area, volume
- Work with time and rates

**Statistics and Data:**
- Interpret graphs, charts, and tables
- Calculate statistical measures (mean, median, mode)
- Understand data distribution
- Draw conclusions from data

**Probability:**
- Interpret situations involving chance
- Calculate simple probabilities
- Understand likelihood and risk

---

## Assessment Criteria

- **Achieved:** Uses mathematics and statistics to meet the numeracy demands of a range of everyday situations
- Graded as "Achieved" or "Not Achieved" only
- No Merit or Excellence grades for unit standards

## Key Numeracy Concepts

### Number Sense

#### Operations:
- Addition and subtraction in context
- Multiplication for repeated groups
- Division for sharing and grouping
- Order of operations (BEDMAS)

#### Fractions, Decimals, Percentages:
- Converting between forms
- Finding percentages of amounts
- Percentage increase/decrease
- Comparing fractions

#### Ratios and Proportions:
- Simplifying ratios
- Scaling recipes or quantities
- Map scales
- Rate problems (speed, cost per unit)

### Measurement

#### Length:
- mm, cm, m, km conversions
- Perimeter calculations

#### Area:
- Square units (cm², m², ha, km²)
- Rectangle, triangle, circle areas
- Composite shapes

#### Volume and Capacity:
- mL, L conversions
- cm³, m³
- Volume of rectangular prisms

#### Mass:
- g, kg, t conversions

#### Time:
- 12-hour and 24-hour time
- Duration calculations
- Time zones

### Statistics

#### Data Types:
- Categorical vs numerical
- Discrete vs continuous

#### Statistical Measures:
- **Mean:** Sum ÷ count (average)
- **Median:** Middle value when ordered
- **Mode:** Most frequent value
- **Range:** Difference between highest and lowest

#### Graphs and Charts:
- Bar graphs
- Pie charts
- Line graphs
- Histograms
- Box plots

#### Interpretation:
- Reading data from graphs
- Identifying trends
- Making predictions
- Drawing conclusions

### Probability

#### Basic Concepts:
- Probability scale (0 to 1)
- Impossible, unlikely, likely, certain
- Experimental vs theoretical probability

#### Calculations:
- Probability = favourable outcomes ÷ total outcomes
- Complementary events (P(not A) = 1 - P(A))
- Combined events

### Geometry

#### Shape Properties:
- Triangles, quadrilaterals, circles
- Angles (acute, right, obtuse, straight, reflex)
- Parallel and perpendicular lines

#### Transformations:
- Reflection
- Rotation
- Translation
- Enlargement

## Problem-Solving Strategies

### RUCSAC Method:
- **R**ead the problem carefully
- **U**nderline key information
- **C**hoose the operation/method
- **S**olve the problem
- **A**nswer the question asked
- **C**heck your answer makes sense

### Estimation:
- Round numbers to check reasonableness
- Use approximations for quick checks
- Sense-check final answers

## Assessment Tips

1. **Read carefully:** Understand what the question asks
2. **Show your working:** Even if answer is wrong, method may be correct
3. **Check units:** Make sure your answer uses correct units
4. **Estimate first:** Get a rough idea of what answer should be
5. **Check reasonableness:** Does your answer make sense in context?
6. **Use a calculator wisely:** Enter numbers correctly, don't rely solely on it

## Common Student Mistakes

### Arithmetic:
- Incorrect order of operations
- Decimal point errors
- Forgetting to convert units
- Calculator entry errors

### Problem Solving:
- Not reading the question fully
- Choosing wrong operation
- Not answering what was asked
- Forgetting to include units

### Statistics:
- Misreading graphs
- Confusing mean, median, mode
- Drawing unsupported conclusions

## NZ Curriculum Alignment

The Numeracy co-requisite aligns with approximately Level 4/5 of the NZ Curriculum (Phase 3/4). Students should:
- Have full control over Phase 3 numeracy skills
- Be beginning to work at Phase 4

## Assessment Information

### CAA Format:
- Digital-first (completed on computer)
- Approximately 60 minutes (no strict time limit in single session)
- Multiple attempts allowed (no maximum resits)
- Available from Year 9 onwards

### Allowed Materials:
- Standard calculator (non-scientific, non-graphing)
- Ruler
- Blank paper for workings (provided by school)
- Headphones for text-to-speech (Polly)

### Not Allowed:
- Phones
- Notes
- Scientific/graphing calculators
- Communication with others
